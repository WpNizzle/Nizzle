Troubleshooting and Problem Solving

Encountering difficulties? This section provides troubleshooting tips for common issues. Discover solutions to error messages, common pitfalls, and workarounds to ensure smooth usage.

Error Message: "Plugin Not Activated"

If you receive this error message, it means the plugin is not activated. Please navigate to the WordPress dashboard, go to the "Plugins" section, and ensure that the plugin is activated. If the issue persists, try deactivating and reactivating the plugin, and clear your browser cache.

Issue: Slow Performance

If you notice slow performance, first ensure that your website and server are properly optimized. Additionally, check if other plugins are causing conflicts. Deactivate non-essential plugins and see if the performance improves. You can also optimize images and implement caching mechanisms to boost speed.

Problem: Broken Styling After Update

If your website's styling breaks after updating the plugin, it might be due to a conflict with your theme or other plugins. Try disabling other plugins one by one to identify the conflicting one. If the issue persists, contact our support team with details about your setup for further assistance.

Error: 404 Page Not Found on Plugin Page

If you encounter a 404 page not found error when accessing a plugin's settings page, ensure that the plugin is properly installed. If the issue persists, it might be a permalink issue. Navigate to "Settings" > "Permalinks" and click "Save Changes" to refresh your permalinks and resolve the issue.
